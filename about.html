The Beginning

A Card Table, a Cessna 150, and $1,000

Howard G. Van Bortel learned to fly in a Cessna 150 on a grass strip in Upstate New York in 1975. The experience was enough to launch a lifelong career: ten years later, in 1985, he founded Van Bortel Aircraft with $1,000 in the bank and a card table set up in his sister's living room.

Howard brought more than three decades of hands-on experience in general aviation to that first sale, and he never compromised on two principles: sell only the absolute best Cessna aircraft, and care for customers like no one else in the industry. That combination turned a $1,000 investment into what is today the world's most recognized, trusted, and respected Cessna dealer.

Growth

From One Hangar to a Propjet Service Center

By 1997, the Van Bortel Aircraft service center had earned a reputation for quality workmanship strong enough that Cessna Aircraft Company awarded it a Propjet Service Center designation — one of only ten in the entire United States. That kind of manufacturer recognition doesn't happen without years of consistent, careful work on customer aircraft.

As the business grew, so did its footprint at Arlington Municipal Airport. Van Bortel Aircraft purchased additional hangars in 2000, 2006, and again in 2012 to keep pace with demand from Cessna owners and buyers across the country. Today the company operates as an FAA Authorized Repair Station (#VNFR171L), a distinction it has held for more than 30 years.

Financing

Van Bortel Finance Corporation

In 2010, Van Bortel Aircraft established Van Bortel Finance Corporation to help customers finance their Cessna and general aviation purchases in-house. The goal was simple: remove one more obstacle between a pilot and the right aircraft, using the same trust-first approach that built the dealership.

Within two years, Van Bortel Finance Corporation had helped finance more than $30,000,000 in Cessna aircraft. For buyers weighing what a trusted dealer relationship actually looks like, an in-house finance arm that understands the aircraft it's lending against is a meaningful part of the answer.

What We Believe

Care, Trust, and the Long View

Van Bortel Aircraft's culture rests on three ideas Howard has repeated since 1985: care about people — customers, vendors, and employees alike; trust people, because trust is what earns trust in return; and think long term, planning around the reputation this business will carry when the next generation is ready to take the reins.

That long-term thinking shapes everything from how aircraft are inspected before sale to how customer relationships are handled decades after a purchase closes. It's also why so many buyers come to Van Bortel Aircraft already thinking about how ownership will change the way they travel for years to come, not just the transaction in front of them.

Recognition

Awards That Reflect the Work

Van Bortel Aircraft has received Cessna's highest honors, including the #1 CSTAR Award in the Nation, the Heavy Hauler Award, Most Skyhawks Sold, Most Skylanes Sold, the Rising Star Award, and the Parts Volume Achievement Award. The company is also an FAA-approved avionics shop with a reputation among pilots for the highest standard of work.

None of those recognitions changes the fundamentals of the business. Van Bortel Aircraft is still, at its core, a Cessna Authorized Single, Multi, and PropJet Service Center that specializes in Independence-built Cessnas and helps buyers find the aircraft that actually fits their mission, one relationship at a time.
